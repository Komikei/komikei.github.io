Valve was founded in 1996 by former longtime Microsoft employees Gabe Newell and Mike Harrington. Wanting to move onto a new venture using their shared wealth, they founded Valve, L.L.C. in Kirkland, Washington on August 24, 1996. After the critical and commercial success of their debut product, the first-person shooter Half-Life, Valve launched Steam in 2003. Steam accounted for around half of digital PC game sales by 2011. By 2012, Valve employed around 250 people and was reportedly worth over US$3 billion, making it the most profitable company per employee in the United States. In the 2010s, Valve began developing hardware, such as the Steam Machine, a brand of gaming PCs, as well as the HTC Vive and Valve Index virtual reality headsets.
